Hello! My name is Darren Yau. I am a graduate student in the Department of Religion at Princeton University, in the religion, ethics, and politics subfield.

I think and write at the intersection of political theory and religious ethics. Broadly, my research focuses on how religious commitments are shaped by and continue to shape political thought. My current interests include questions such as:

  • How do we make sense of the emergence of non-violent resistance within traditions of theological reflection about violence, just war, self-defense, peace, and resistance?
  • How do figures within the Christian theological tradition defend or dissent from liberal accounts of political authority?
  • How do theological commitments alter accounts of property rights?
  • What are the theological dimensions of the practices of blame and redress, such as reparations and collective acts of repentance?
